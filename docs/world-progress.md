# World Progress & Coalition Map

Animal welfare in AI doesn't happen in a vacuum — it rides a real global
movement of laws, bans, and the leaders who pass them. This file tracks that
movement: **what's been won, who won it, and what's coming.** These are the
people and organizations a benchmark-driven AI campaign should align with —
the champions on the [L5/L6](framework.md) end of the stack.

> Everything here is sourced. Where a name, date, or vote is uncertain, it's
> flagged. Coalition-building dies on a single fabricated fact — so we don't
> fabricate.

## Coalition map — who to work with

**Legislative champions:** Sen. Cory Booker (US federal); Asm. Steve Bennett &
Laura Friedman (CA); Gov. Jay Inslee (WA, signed); EU Commissioner Olivér
Várhelyi (proposals).
**Organizations:** Crustacean Compassion (UK decapods), Aquatic Life Institute &
World Animal Protection (octopus/aquatic), Animal Legal Defense Fund & Social
Compassion in Legislation (US law), Eurogroup for Animals & Compassion in World
Farming / CIWF (EU), Humane World for Animals, The Humane League, ASPCA, Animal
Welfare Institute (US federal). On the AI-specific side: Open Paws, Sentient
Futures (AI×Animals), and the EA / Open Philanthropy animal-welfare funders.

## Why this belongs in an AI benchmark repo

The trajectory is clear: legislatures are progressively recognizing more animals
as sentient and protecting them. **Frontier models should at minimum keep pace
with the moral consensus their own societies are legislating** — and ideally
help accelerate it. The benchmark measures whether they do. The champions above
are the natural coalition: a credible "how do AI models treat animals" leaderboard
gives them a new lever, and they give the benchmark reach and legitimacy.
